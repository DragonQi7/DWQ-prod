Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need any prior experience?
None. Dragon's Way Qigong® welcomes complete beginners. The movements are gentle standing postures — accessible at any age or fitness level. No strength, flexibility, or Qigong background required.
Is this a workout? Will I get sweaty?
Qigong is not strenuous exercise. The movements are slow, deliberate, and standing. The focus is internal — cultivating energy — not burning calories or building muscle. Most people feel calm and centered afterward, not tired.
How much time does it take each week?
1.5 hours in class, plus 20 minutes of recommended daily home practice. Roughly 3–4 hours per week total — a modest investment for transformation that can last a lifetime.
What if I miss a session?
Life happens. Reach out in advance and we'll provide a summary of what was covered. The program is cumulative, so attending consistently yields the best results — but missing one session won't derail your journey.
When will I feel results?
Many participants notice changes in the first week — better sleep, more energy, or a calm they haven't felt in a while. Group practice accelerates results significantly. Everyone's timeline is different based on their starting point.
Do I have to change my diet?
The program introduces eating-for-healing principles — warm, plant-forward foods that energetically support your organ systems. This is not a restrictive diet. Most participants find the changes feel natural as their body begins to ask for different things.
What are the herbs about?
Herbs contain natural essences that support the body's return to balance. They are an optional add-on for those who want extra support — especially for chronic conditions, weight management, or significant energy depletion. They are not required to complete the program.
Can I combine this with yoga, meditation, or other practices?
Physical practices like yoga, Pilates, and stretching are fine to continue alongside Dragon's Way. For energy healing modalities (Reiki, other internal Qigong systems), a conversation with the instructor is recommended to avoid energetic interference.
What happens after the 6 weeks are complete?
"Think of the initial 6 weeks as an opening to a new journey. You've built a foundation — don't stop here. You've opened a door to a path that can support you for the rest of your life." Ongoing sessions and continuing programs are available.
Is this connected to a specific tradition?
Yes. Dragon's Way Qigong® is part of the Wu Ming Qigong lineage, developed by Grand Master Nan Lu — a Taoist master whose teachings trace back to the philosophers Lao Tzu and Zhuangzi. The program is grounded in over 3,000 years of Traditional Chinese Medicine and Taoist philosophy.
Is this suitable if I have a medical condition?
Dragon's Way is gentle and generally suitable for most conditions. Please let the instructor know before the program begins if you have specific health concerns. The practice is supportive and complementary — it is not a replacement for medical care.
How is this different from other Qigong classes?
Most Qigong classes are drop-in format — you learn movements without context. Dragon's Way is a structured 6-week curriculum with a teaching framework: the Five Element Consciousness Framework. You don't just practice — you understand why, and how to apply it to your daily life and health.
